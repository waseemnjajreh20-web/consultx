This section contains specific requirements for 
the construction of gas cabinets that contain 
medical gases in quantities requiring a permit 
under Section 105 of SBC 801. These 
requirements are in addition to those contained 
in Section 5003.8.6 of SBC 801. The cabinet 
ventilation system must be designed to operate 
at a negative pressure and be connected to an 
exhaust system to maintain a safe atmosphere 
inside the room in the event of a leak, 
especially during the normal opening and 
closing of the cabinet. Additionally, gas 
cabinets must be protected with an internal 
automatic sprinkler system to cool the gas 
cylinders housed in the cabinet in the event of 
a fire outside of the cabinet. Other types of 
extinguishing systems are prohibited. 
SECTION 428 HIGHER EDUCATION 
LABORATORIES 
428.1  Scope. Higher education laboratories 
complying with the requirements of Sections 428.1  
through 428.4 shall be permitted to exceed the 
maximum allowable quantities of hazardous 
materials in control areas set forth in Table 307.1(1) 
and Table 307.1(2) without requiring classification 
as a Group H occupancy. Except as specified in 
Section 428, such laboratories shall comply with all 
applicable provisions of this code and SBC 801. 
❖ 
Section 428, in conjunction with Chapter 38 of 
SBC 801, provides a design option for higher 
education facilities that need to exceed the 
standard control area limitations found in 
Section 414 and Chapter 50 of SBC 801. This 
section allows for moderate increases in 
hazardous materials storage and use but 
imposes 
additional 
construction 
and 
operational controls in exchange for the 
increased allowances. It is very important to 
note that this section does not automatically 
apply to any new or existing higher education 
facilities. Only facilities that want to use the 
increased allowances, and can meet the 
additional requirements, would need to comply 
with this section. 
428.2  Application.  The provisions of Section 
428 shall be applied as exceptions or additions to 
applicable requirements of this code. Unless 
specifically modified by Section 428, the storage, 
use and handling of hazardous materials shall 
comply with all other provisions in Chapters 38 and 
50 through 67 of SBC 801 and this code for 
quantities not exceeding the maximum allowable 
quantity. 
❖ 
Higher education facilities still need to comply 
with all other requirements of this code and with 
Chapters 50 through 67 of SBC 801 except as 
modified by Sections 428.3  and 428.4 . 
428.3  Laboratory suite construction.  Where 
laboratory suites are provided, they shall be 
constructed in accordance with this section and 
Chapter 38 of SBC 801. The number of laboratory 
suites and percentage of maximum allowable 
quantities of hazardous materials in laboratory 
suites shall be in accordance with Table 428.3. 
❖ 
When utilizing the allowances of Section 428, 
the building will be designed with laboratory 
suites which are very similar in concept to 
control areas. Chapter 38 of SBC 801 also 
contains detailed requirements for laboratory 
suite construction that are correlated. Users 
must utilize both codes when determining 
requirements for construction of laboratory 
suites. 
 
Laboratory suites provide a much higher level 
of fire and hazardous materials protection than 
the traditional control area design described in 
Section 
414. 
Besides 
the 
additional 
requirements found in this section, laboratory 
suites are required to have: 
• 
Rated fire barriers for compartmentation of 
laboratory suites within buildings. 
• 
Standby or emergency power for safety-
related equipment and enhanced automatic 
sprinkler protection in laboratories above the 
sixth story or in a story below grade plane. 
• 
Automatic sprinkler design and density 
exceeding that which would be required by 
NFPA 45. 
 
See Table 428.3. 
❖ 
Table 428.3 identifies the number of laboratory 
suites allowed per floor in a building. The table 
then identifies the percentage of MAQ that is 
allowed in each laboratory suite. This table 
supports the design concept that is nearly 
identical to the control area concept in the 
codes.

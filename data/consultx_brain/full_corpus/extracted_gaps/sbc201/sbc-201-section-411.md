410.7  Standpipes. Standpipe systems shall be 
provided in accordance with Section 905.  
❖ 
Because of the potentially large fuel load and 
three-dimensional aspect of the fire hazard 
associated with stages greater than 100 m2 in 
area, a Class III wet standpipe system is 
required on each side of such stages. The 
standpipes are required to be equipped with 
both a 40 mm and 65 mm hose connection. 
The required design criteria for the standpipe 
system, including hoses and cabinets, is 
specified in Section 905.3.4 .  
SECTION 411 SPECIAL AMUSEMENT 
AREAS 
411.1  General. Special amusement areas having 
an occupant load of 50 or more shall comply with 
the requirements for the appropriate Group A 
occupancy and Sections 411.1 through 411.7 . 
Special amusement areas having an occupant load 
of less than 50 shall comply with the requirements 
for a Group B occupancy and Sections 411.1 
through 411.7 .  
Exception: Special amusement areas that are 
without walls or a roof and constructed to prevent 
the accumulation of smoke need not comply with 
this section.  
❖ 
A special amusement area is one where the 
egress is not readily apparent, is intentionally 
confounded or is not readily available (see 
Section 202). This section addresses the 
hazards associated with such use by requiring 
automatic fire detection (see Section 411.2 ), 
automatic sprinkler protection (see Section 
411.3 ), alarm requirements (see Section 411.4 
), an emergency voice/alarm communications 
system (see Section 411.5 ) and specific 
means of egress lighting and marking (see 
Section 411.6 ). Additionally, only interior finish 
materials that meet the most stringent flame 
spread classification, Class A, are permitted in 
special amusement areas (see Section 411.7 ). 
 
In addition to the provisions of this section, 
other applicable requirements, such as means 
of egress (i.e., occupant load and travel 
distance) and building construction (i.e., type of 
construction, and fire-resistance ratings), apply 
in accordance with the appropriate assembly 
group classification (see Section 303.1 ).  
 
Special amusement areas are considered 
assembly uses based on the provisions of 
Section 303.1. Section 303.1 further specifies 
that a Group A classification is applicable only 
where the space or building's design occupant 
load is 50 or more. The provisions of Section 
411 apply in addition to the other requirements 
for the appropriate assembly use, usually 
Group A-1 or A-3. Very small special 
amusement areas are not required to be 
classified as Group A. Where the design 
occupant load is less than 50, then the space 
or building is classified as Group B. This 
acknowledges the lesser hazards associated 
with smaller groups of people. Although 
smaller buildings are classified as Group B, 
they must still meet the requirements of this 
section as special amusement areas.  
 
Section 411 does not apply to a facility that is 
constructed to permit the free and immediate 
ventilation of the products of combustion to the 
outside. Such free and immediate ventilation 
addresses the hazard associated with many 
special amusement buildings relative to the 
rapid accumulation of smoke in a building in 
which the egress is not readily apparent, 
confusing or not readily available.  
 
All flammable decorative materials used in 
special amusement areas are required to 
follow the provisions of SBC 801. The use of 
flammable and combustible materials in these 
types of buildings must be strictly regulated to 
limit the fire hazards to the building occupants 
(refer to Chapter 8 of SBC 801 for those 
restrictions).  
411.2  Automatic sprinkler system. Buildings 
containing special amusement areas shall be 
equipped throughout with an automatic sprinkler 
system in accordance with Section 903.3.1.1. 
Where the special amusement area is temporary, the 
sprinkler water supply shall be of an approved 
temporary means.  
Exception: Automatic sprinklers are not required 
where the total floor area of a temporary special 
amusement area is less than 100 m2 and the exit 
access travel distance from any point in the special 
amusement area to an exit is less than 15 m.

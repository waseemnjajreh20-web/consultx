SECTION 506 BUILDING AREA 
506.1  General.  The floor area of a building shall 
be determined based on the type of construction, 
occupancy classification, whether there is an 
automatic sprinkler system installed throughout the 
building and the amount of building frontage on 
public way or open space.  
❖ 
This 
section 
is 
a 
fairly 
straightforward 
statement regarding which attributes of a 
building impact the allowable area of a building. 
These attributes are the type of construction, 
as described in Chapter 6; the occupancy 
classification of the building, as described in 
Chapter 3; the presence, or lack thereof, of an 
automatic sprinkler system for the entire 
building; and the amount of the building that 
has frontage on an open space or public way. 
This section is simply a charging statement for 
what is to come. The limitations on numbers of 
stories and meter are found in Sections 504.3 
and 504.4 .  
506.1.1  Unlimited area buildings. Unlimited area 
buildings shall be designed in accordance with 
Section 507.  
-

❖ 
Section 507 allows certain buildings to be 
unlimited in area due to lack of exposure, low 
hazard level, construction type, the presence of 
fire safety systems or a combination of these 
characteristics. 
Under 
these 
specific 
provisions, Section 506.2 does not apply 
unless referenced. See also Section 503.1.3 
for additional options for unlimited area 
buildings.  
506.1.2  Special provisions.  The special provisions 
of Section 510 permit the use of special conditions 
that are exempt from, or modify, the specific 
requirements of this chapter regarding the allowable 
areas of buildings based on the occupancy 
classification and type of construction, provided the 
special condition complies with the provisions 
specified in Section 510.  
❖ 
Section 510 allows certain buildings with 
additional safeguards to adjust the heights and 
areas allowed by Section 506.2 . Many of the 
provisions allow a mixture of construction 
types, and most involve a distinct portion of the 
structure being used for parking.  
506.1.3  Basements.  Basements need not be 
included in the total allowable floor area of a 
building provided the total area of such basements 
does not exceed the area permitted for a one-story 
above grade plane building. 
❖ 
Assuming the basement is the same footprint 
as the building, the area of a single basement 
is not required to be counted as part of the total 
building area when evaluating total allowable 
area in accordance with Section 506.2 . 
Determining the maximum area for the 
basement can include the frontage increase in 
Section 506.3 . Where there are multiple 
basements, the area of the basements is 
combined in order to determine if the 
basements will be considered to count toward 
the limits for the total permitted building area. 
Therefore, if the allowable area of a single-
story building is 4645 m2 in accordance with 
Sections 506.2 and 506.3 , the total aggregate 
area of all stories below grade plane must not 
exceed 4645 m2. If the aggregate area exceeds 
that allowed for a single-story building, the 
basement 
levels 
must 
be 
counted 
as 
contributing to the total allowable area for the 
building.  
506.1.3.1  
Basement requirements.  It shall 
be  permissible to construct one or more basement 
floors covering the entire area of the land, provided 
that all technical, architectural, and structural 
requirements related to public safety, ventilation 
systems, groundwater drainage, and sewage are 
met, in accordance with the relevant executive 
regulations and environmental requirements 
506.2  Allowable area 
determination. 
The 
allowable area of a building shall be determined in 
accordance with the applicable provisions of 
Sections 506.2.1 , 506.2.2 and Section 506.3   
❖ 
The process for determining the allowable area 
of a building is provided in Sections 506.2.1, 
506.2.2 and 506.3. At the core of the 
requirements is Table 506.2, which provides 
tabular area values that are plugged into the 
applicable equations in Sections 506.2.1 and 
506.2.2. The tabular values are established 
based on the type of construction, the 
occupancy classification and whether the 
building is equipped throughout with an 
automatic sprinkler system. The allowable area 
values are then determined based on these 
tabular values, the number of stories and the 
amount of frontage the building has on open 
space or a public way.  

See Table 506.2 

The use of Table 506.2 is explained in the 
example in Section 506.2. Regarding the Notes 
a through h, see the commentary provided on 
the notes for Table 504.3. Note i allows 
nonsprinklered greenhouses that fall into a 
Group U classification to have a maximum area 
of 835 m2. If a larger size is desired, the note 
directs the code user to Appendix C. 

Significant updates are made to Table 506.2 to 
address new construction Types IV-A, IV-B 
and IV-C. Although these new construction 
types are introduced with fire-resistance rating 
requirements that approximate Types I-A and 
I-B construction, due to the combustibility and 
relatively short history of mass timber, these 
new construction types are provided with 
limitations to area, unlike most occupancies of 
Type I construction that are allowed unlimited 
area. Also, buildings of Type I construction do 
not require any restriction on the size per floor 
as building height increases, but with Type IV 
construction, since there is an upper limit to the 
total building area, the average allowable area 
per floor is decreased as the building height in 
stories increase. This results in decreased 
horizontal travel distance to exit stairs and 
decreased distance to emergency situations 
for responding emergency personnel in taller 
Type IV buildings. It is worth noting that 
conservatism is achieved as there is no 
increased areas permitted in the NS rows for 
Types IV-A, IV-B and IV-C beyond what is 
allowed for Type IV-HT, even with the added 
fire 
resistance 
and/or 
noncombustible 
protection requirements.

This Section gives the basic formulas for 
determining the allowable area for single-
occupancy buildings. Equation 5-1 is used to 
determine the allowable area of each floor in 
buildings up to three stories, while Equation 5-
2 determines the total allowable area of a 
building greater than three stories. Basically, 
the allowable area per floor of a single-
occupancy building is based on the tabular 
area of Table 506.2 added to the product of the 
tabular area for a nonsprinklered building and 
the frontage increase factor per Section 
506.3.3 . In buildings taller than three stories, 
that value is multiplied by three to find the total 
allowable area for the building or multiplied by 
four if the building has an NFPA 13R sprinkler 
system. Finally, no individual story can be 
greater than the allowable area of a one-story 
building, as illustrated in Examples 4 and 5.  

Example 1: A one-story motel, planned to be 
Type IIIB construction. As required by the 
code, the building will be equipped throughout 
with an NFPA 13R sprinkler system. The 
building has less than 25 percent of its 
perimeter facing a public way or open space. 
What is the allowable area?  

Answer: The allowable area for this building is 
1485 m2.  

Explanation: The occupancy classification for 
a motel is R-1. Under the column Type III, 
Column B, in the row for R-1, S13R, the value 
for At is 1485 m2, and NS = 1485 m2.  

In Equation 5-1, If = 0. (See commentary on 
Section 506.3 for a detailed explanation of If) 
Therefore, in Equation 5-1: Aa = 1485 + (1485 
x 0) = 1485 m2.  

Observe that, for residential occupancies, 
there is no advantage to having a sprinkler 
system installed in accordance with NFPA 13R 
versus having no sprinkler system at all. 
However, for this same building, the allowable 
area would be 5950 m2 if an NFPA 13 sprinkler 
system were installed. 

Example 2: A one-story retail store building is 
proposed to be built 1950 m2 in area. Like 
Example 1, the building has only 25 percent of 
its perimeter facing a public way or open space. 
What is the minimum type of construction 
allowed for this building? If an NFPA 13 
sprinkler system was to be installed, what 
would be the minimum type of construction 
allowed?  

Answer: The minimum type of construction for 
this building without a sprinkler system would 
be Type IIA. With an NFPA 13 sprinkler 
system, the minimum type of construction 
would be Type VB.

The allowable area of a building with more than 
one occupancy is dependent on the option 
used in Section 508 for multiple occupancies. 
The commentary to Section 508 addresses this 
in some detail. The code provides three basic 
options for mixed occupancies: accessory 
occupancies, nonseparated occupancies and 
separated occupancies. The availability of the 
options depends on the area of each story 
desired for each different occupancy and the 
relationship of one occupancy to the other. For 
mixed-occupancy buildings up to three stories 
above grade, the allowable area for each floor 
is determined by either Section 508.3.2 for 
nonseparated occupancies or Section 508.4.2 
for separated occupancies.
